Problem Solving

1. were blocked on a simple problem: what was the problem? what problem-solving techniques did you use? how did you feel throughout the process? what did you learn?

Stupid things like spelling mistakes (ie capitalization/camelCasing/case sensitivity) were easy issues that I came across that were easily solved by going through line by line to see if there were any simple issues parsing in variable names and confusing naming conventions of object values and variables.

2. elegantly solved a problem. what was the problem? what problem-solving techniques did you use? how did you feel throughout the process? what did you learn?

Breaking down problems into subproblems was immensely useful and then assessing/comparing these subproblems waiting to be solved against the corpus of previous code (functions/subroutines/declared variables etc) completed. Trying to see what I have previously solved and whether the solutions conceived is applicable towards new problems was immensely useful to my learning as well as finding out the blindspots in my knowledge that needed to be supplimented through googling out extra solutions etc etc.

3. Reflect on how confident you feel using each of these problem-solving techniques/processes:

Pseudocode

Pseudocode is useful insofar as providing a guideline about how to go about solving a problem. Planning ahead of time as to what you need to tackle and preempting known issues that require solving gives you a plan to use other problemsolving tools and techniques as per the following...

Trying something

Trying something and throwing things up to the wall can be useful insofar as having some sort of instructive guide or parameters of action to use against. One can be easily led on a goose chase if you just go in blind without some sort of goal or objective in sight. Knowing what to try is 90% more useful than throwing spaghetti at the walls.

Reading error messages

Really useful in conjunction with googling StackOverflow threads as the errors raised within the console.log presents great learning opportunities and points towards quirks and design architecture that's endemic towards a programming language paradigm. Continual errors I kept coming against were in regards to certain inbuilt methods being only applicable to specific data types (ie the inability to push objects, the differences between dot and bracket notations which effected the types of methods I could use for certain problems and challenges)

Asking your peers for help, Asking coaches for help, Improving your process with reflection

Peers, colleagues and Coaches are all useful resources to ask for assistance/help, as well as to help clarify ones own understanding or knowledge on a topic as it presents an opportunity for us to test our own knowledge and understanding. Being able to come and break through this mental roadblock of reaching out can be difficult (i.e. how much toil and self work must one do before we should reach out is a question one is constantly asking oneself). It can be issue as fundamentally we are all responsible for our own self-learning (trying to walk this tightrope can be an issue?)

Foundation Reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

The importance and awareness of neuroplasticity within the learning processing was quite illuminating i.e. realizing that the need to strengthen muscle memory as a means to improve one's coding ability.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Fundamentally, programming is a collaborative exercise where contributers delegated different tasks and respobsibilities. Being aware of how to function as a team requires high levels of empathy and self-awareness in the way you treat yourself and others but also in how you teach yourself and others. Learning can be difficult, so to remind yourself to be kind is an important skill to cultivate.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Putting onto paper, ways of learning that are intuitively understood about how to learn technical skills. The explanations given in the core modules like neuroplasticity and how a greater understanding of brain or cognitive science can enable one to make more efficient ones learning was a pleasant suprise.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Some of the content can be hairy-fairy or newagey, I think overcoming these prejudices might be a barrier. To be honest, the most challenging aspects of core learning at the moment is the lack of team work we've had so far in applying some of this knowledge.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

As reiterated above, programming is often a collaborative endeavour and to be able to function successfully within this environment requires high level EQ in order to help negotiate personal relationships within this dynamic.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I feel perhaps there has not so much application of the core learning within the foundations stage of the course (though I imagine in due course this learning will be applied). Core learning didnt' feel like a waste of time at all.

Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence

Emotional Intelligence (EQ) relates to competencies/ability to to perceive, control, evaluate, and express emotions. The importance of high EQ therefore helps you to build relationships, reduce team stress, defuse conflict and improve job satisfaction

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ (or emotional intelligence quotient) can be described as a person's ability to perceive, control, evaluate, and express emotions. whilst cognitive intelligence (IQ) can be best described as metric to be described knowledge concerning Visual and spatial processing, Visual and spatial processing, Working memory and short-term memory, Quantitative reasoning

Why is EQ important?

The need to have a high EQ is important insofar as helping one negotiate and best build relationships. As important as IQ is, people are fundamentally social creatures who thrive working collaboratively and in groups - more especially important today given the diversity of specialities and expert knowledge required in todays modern economy, the ability to negotiate and understand group dynamics is more important than ever insofar as helping to coordinate and plan how one is able to deliver on projects, products, events or things...

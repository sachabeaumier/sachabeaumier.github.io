Foundation Reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

The importance and awareness of neuroplasticity within the learning processing was quite illuminating i.e. realizing that the need to strengthen muscle memory as a means to improve one's coding ability. Another thing of importance learnt is the role of EQ within the domain of software development and engineering (seemed obvious that this is an important skill to build on). Final thing I learnt were new research skills as to it relate to troubleshooting/errorlogging problems I may encounter in the building of programs. Learning how to use stackOverflow and other online resources in a constructive way is a definite plus in regards to my core learning.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Fundamentally, programming is a collaborative exercise where contributers delegated different tasks and responsibilities. Being aware of how to function as a team requires high levels of empathy and self-awareness in the ways one treats one's self and others, but also in how you teach yourself and others. Learning can be difficult, so to remind yourself to be kind is an important skill to cultivate. The importance of values shouldn't be understated. Having a set of values and core beliefs are important to ground one's empathy as it is an important basis for enactment of empathy within a sincere and grounded manner.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Putting onto paper, ways of learning that are intuitively understood about how to learn technical skills. The explanations given in the core modules like neuroplasticity and how a greater understanding of brain or cognitive science can enable one to make more efficient ones learning was a pleasant suprise.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Some of the content can be hairy-fairy or newagey, I think overcoming these prejudices might be a barrier. To be honest, the most challenging aspects of core learning at the moment is the lack of team work we've had so far in applying some of this knowledge (this I imagine will change once we head into bootcamp).

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

As reiterated above, programming is often a collaborative endeavour and to be able to function successfully within this environment requires high level EQ in order to help negotiate personal relationships within this dynamic.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I feel perhaps there has not so much application of the core learning within the foundations stage of the course (though I imagine in due course this learning will be applied). Core learning didnt' feel like a waste of time at all.
